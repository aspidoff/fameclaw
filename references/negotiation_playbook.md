# FameClaw Negotiation Playbook

When an influencer replies to outreach, the conversation moves to **NEGOTIATE** stage. This playbook guides the agent (or user) through the negotiation flow.

---

## Phase 1: Discovery (First Reply)

When the creator responds, **don't pitch yet**. Gather intel first.

### Ask the creator:
1. **Demographics** — "Could you share your audience demographics? Specifically top countries and age/gender breakdown."
2. **Rates** — "What are your rates for a video integration? And do you offer volume discounts for multiple videos?"
3. **Past work** — "Do you have examples of past brand collaborations? Would love to see how you integrate sponsors."
4. **Preferred format** — Let them tell you what they're comfortable with (dedicated video, mid-roll integration, newsletter, shorts, etc.)

### Ask the brand owner (user):
Before making any offer, the agent should clarify with the user:
1. **Budget range** — "What's your max budget per creator? And your ideal price?"
2. **Negotiation style** — "How aggressive should I negotiate? Options:"
   - **Friendly** — meet their price if reasonable, prioritize relationship
   - **Value-focused** — always counter, propose creative structures, anchor low
   - **Budget-strict** — hard cap, walk away if over budget
3. **Deal-breakers** — "Any hard no's?" (e.g. minimum subscriber count, no competing products, specific countries only)
4. **Payment terms preference** — 50/50, full upfront, net-30, performance-based?

---

## Phase 2: Evaluation

Once you have demographics + rates, evaluate before countering:

### Red flags (deprioritize):
- Creator sells **competing products** through their channel (they'll always price you out)
- Audience is **wrong geography** (>50% outside target markets)
- Demographics don't match brand's target buyer
- Creator has **no past brand work** and wants premium rates
- Engagement rate is suspiciously low vs subscriber count

### Green flags (push harder):
- Audience geography matches target market
- Creator has done similar integrations before (proven format)
- They responded quickly and seem eager
- Their rates are within or near budget
- **Creator proposes their own video idea** — this is the best signal. They're already thinking about how to make it work. Close fast.
- Creator has a **relevant video already in production** — perfect natural integration opportunity

---

## Phase 3: Counter-Offer

### Tactics (learned from real negotiations):

**1. Anchor low, climb slowly**
- Start at 60-70% of their asking price
- Frame as "first of many" — implies ongoing revenue stream
- Only bump in small increments after pushback

**2. Propose creative structure**
- Instead of 1 big video → offer 3 × short integrations for same total
- More touches = better ROI for brand, same money for creator
- "What about 3 × 60-second mid-roll integrations across your next videos for $X?"

**3. Bundle multiple channels**
- If creator has YouTube + newsletter + Discord → bundle for discount
- "Could we do a package? 2 videos + 4 newsletters for $X?"

**4. 50/50 payment terms**
- "50% upfront, 50% after video is done (before publishing)"
- Reduces creator risk, protects brand from non-delivery
- Most creators accept this

**5. Volume discount framing**
- "If this first one performs well, we'd want to do 3-6 more this quarter"
- Creators discount when they see recurring revenue potential

**6. Never name your max first**
- Always ask "What are your rates?" before proposing
- If they name a price, you counter. If you name first, you anchor yourself.

**7. Close fast when price is right**
- If their rate is within budget, don't counter — say "we have a deal" immediately
- Speed builds trust and locks in before they reconsider
- Send payment same day if possible

**8. Stack value with affiliate/rev-share**
- Offer affiliate program on top of flat fee
- Creates ongoing passive income for creator beyond the one-time payment
- Makes a lower flat fee more attractive

**9. Request demographics BEFORE agreeing on price**
- Ask for audience breakdown (countries, age, gender) during discovery
- Use it to justify your counter-offer if demographics are weak
- Never renegotiate after verbal agreement — it damages trust

**10. Script review before recording**
- Always request to review the script/talking points before they record
- Ensures messaging accuracy, prevents surprises
- Professional workflow that protects both sides

**11. Build performance terms into the initial deal**
- "Base fee + bonus if video hits X views in 30 days"
- Aligns creator incentives with brand goals
- Never try to add performance terms after delivery — creator will refuse

---

## Phase 4: Follow-Up Cadence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 0 | Send counter-offer |
| 3 | Gentle follow-up if no reply |
| 7 | Second follow-up — "Still interested?" |
| 14 | Final touch — "No worries if timing isn't right. Happy to revisit later." |
| 90-180 | **Re-engage** — fresh approach, don't reference old negotiation |

**Rules:**
- Max 3 follow-ups per negotiation round
- Never send more than 1 email per day
- If creator says "no" explicitly → respect it, mark as DECLINED
- If creator ghosts → mark as STALE after 14 days, re-engage in 3-6 months

---

## Phase 5: Closing

When both sides agree on terms:

1. **Ask for payment method immediately** — "Is PayPal/Stripe ok?" Don't wait until the deal is done to figure out logistics. Accept multiple options (PayPal, Stripe, Wise).
2. **Agree on exact topic/title** — do NOT send payment until both sides agree on what's being made. If the creator's proposed topic doesn't match your goals, redirect before closing.
3. **Recap in writing** — "Just to confirm: [topic/title], [format], [$amount], [payment terms], [deadline], [tracking link]. Reply 'confirmed' to proceed."
4. **Get explicit confirmation** — creator must reply "yes" or "confirmed"
5. **Set up tracking** — create referral link (e.g. `?r=creatorhandle`) before payment
6. **Milestone-based payment** — "50% on script/draft approval, 50% before publishing." Never pay 50% upfront before seeing any deliverable.
7. **For deals >$500** — send a summary email that reads like a simple contract. Includes: topic, format, deliverable, deadline, payment terms, refund clause if not delivered.
8. **Track delivery** — mark contact as ACTIVE_DEAL in campaign state
9. **Respond within 24 hours** during the close phase — eager creators lose interest fast

### If creator ghosts after payment:
1. Follow up 3x over 2 weeks
2. Send formal email: "We need the deliverable by [date] or a refund of $X"
3. If no response after 30 days, mark as LOST and note for future reference

## Decision Tree

```
Creator replies
  ├── Positive ("yes, interested")
  │   ├── Ask: demographics, rates, past work
  │   ├── Ask user: budget, negotiation style
  │   ├── Evaluate red/green flags
  │   ├── Counter-offer (anchor low)
  │   ├── Follow-up cadence if no reply
  │   └── Close deal or walk away
  │
  ├── Redirect ("I don't do X, but I do Y")
  │   ├── Pivot to their preferred format
  │   ├── Ask same discovery questions for new format
  │   └── Continue negotiation on their terms
  │
  ├── Price too high / competing product
  │   ├── Counter once with creative structure
  │   ├── If still no → park as STALE
  │   └── Re-engage in 3-6 months
  │
  └── Explicit "no"
      └── Mark DECLINED, respect it
```

## Email Templates

### Discovery reply (after creator shows interest):
```
Hey [first_name],

Great to hear you're open to this!

A few quick questions so we can put something together:
1. Could you share your audience demographics (top countries, age/gender)?
2. What are your rates for a [video integration / sponsored post]? Volume discounts?
3. Do you have examples of past brand collabs?

Happy to work around whatever format you're most comfortable with.

[sender_name]
```

### Counter-offer:
```
Hey [first_name],

Thanks for the breakdown. Your audience is a strong fit.

Here's what we're thinking: [X] × [format] for $[amount]. This would be the first in what we hope is an ongoing partnership — if this performs well, we'd want to do [Y] more this quarter.

Payment: 50% upfront, 50% after delivery (before publishing).

What do you think?

[sender_name]
```

### Follow-up (day 3):
```
Hey [first_name],

Just circling back on the above. Let me know your thoughts — happy to adjust if needed.

[sender_name]
```

### Walk-away (respectful):
```
Hey [first_name],

Totally understand — sounds like the timing/budget isn't right for now. No hard feelings at all.

If anything changes down the road, we'd love to revisit. Keep making great content!

[sender_name]
```

### Re-engage (3-6 months later):
```
Hey [first_name],

[sender_name] here from [brand]. Been a while!

Saw your recent video "[video_title]" — really solid stuff. Are you open to any brand collaborations right now?

[sender_name]
```
